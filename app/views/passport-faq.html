Frequently Asked Questions

1. About us

The General Register Office (GRO) is based in Southport, Merseyside and holds records for all births, deaths, marriages and civil partnerships that have been registered in England and Wales from 1837.

2. Records available

Ordering via our online service The following can be ordered through our GRO online ordering service -

  • Certificates for birth, death, marriage, civil partnership and adoption events registered in England and Wales and for certain British Nationals, who were born, married, formed a civil partnership or died overseas.

  • Commemorative marriage certificates for silver, ruby, golden and diamond wedding anniversaries.

  • PDFs for historic digitised civil registration records held by GRO (birth entries from 1837 to up to 100 years ago and death entries from 1837 to 1957).

  • Online View digital image for historic, digitised civil registration records held by GRO (birth entries from 1837 up to 100 years ago and death entries from 1837 - 1957).

  • To order a PDF or Online View digital image, you will need to provide a GRO index reference number. At the point of ordering, you will be taken to the GRO online indexes so that you can search for the reference number for the certificate that you are interested in. If you are unable to trace the record you can still apply for a certificate however there will be an additional administration charge if a GRO index reference is not provided.

The GRO online indexes should be used to try and identify the index reference for: Birth records from 1837 to 1934, and 1984 up to two years ago. Death records from 1837 to 1957, and 1984 up to two years ago in England and Wales. Alternative index sources include https://www.freebmd.org.uk/ and the host sites.

You will be prompted to supply further details for more recent births and deaths (within the last 50 years) and marriages and civil partnerships (within the last 35 years) when you complete the online application form.

If you are unable to complete the mandatory fields, you will not be able to apply online and you will need to follow our telephone ordering process which incurs an additional fee of £4.00 Please contact us on +44(0) 300 123 1837 for further information.

Ordering offline (by telephone or post) only

The following certificates are not available to order online and are not subject to the telephone or postal certificate application fee.

  • Short or abridged versions of certificates (apart from short adoption certificates, which can be ordered online)

  • A certificate from the Register of Presumed Deaths

  • A certificate from the Abandoned Children's Register

  • A certificate from the Parental Order Register and

  • A certificate from the Gender Recognition Marriage or Civil Partnership Registers

If you require a copy of a short birth certificate, or if you require a certificate from the Abandoned Children's Register or the Parental Order Register please contact us on +44(0) 300 123 1837 for further information. For certificates from the Gender Recognition Register, once the registration has been made, you will have been given a direct line to telephone the team or you can use the above number and be redirected.

Records we are not able to provide

GRO is not able to issue certificates for birth or death registrations that have taken place within the last six months and you may wish to obtain a certificate from the register office or church where the event was registered.

This information excludes adoption certificates, as the local register office does not hold a copy of the Adopted Children Register. If you require further information, please contact us on +44(0) 300 123 1837 and talk to our Customer Service Team.

4. Our refund policy

Please note that we will retain a fee of £3.50 (for certificate orders) to cover administration costs incurred if we are unable to find a record of the event you have requested. Any additional administration fees which you may have paid (if not applying using our online service or including a GRO index reference) are also non-refundable.

5. What to do if you have not received your certificate

Please allow at least 5 working days (working days exclude Saturday/Sunday/Bank Holidays) from the estimated date of despatch for delivery before you contact us or a minimum of 15 working days if you live overseas. We will not be able to take action if you contact us before these timescales. If you still haven't received your certificate after this time, please use our online form to send us a message. When you submit this form it will generate an email to us with the details you have supplied. We will respond to your email within 5 working days.

Please note that you should report the non-receipt of a certificate within three months of the date the order was placed.

6. What to do if you have not received your attached PDF?

Please allow 5 working days from the estimated date of order before you contact us. We will not be able to take action if you contact us before these timescales. If you still haven't received your PDF after this time, please use our online form to send us a message. When you submit this form, it will generate an email to us with the details you have supplied. We will respond to your email within 5 working days.

7. What to do if you have not received your Online View digital image?

12. Other services we provide

PDF

Portable Document Format (PDF) is a file format used to present and exchange documents reliably and in a manner that is independent of user's software, hardware, or operating system. PDF's are available for historical digitised civil registration records held by GRO (birth entries from 1837 to 1923 and death entries from 1837 to 1957). Orders for a PDF copy of a registration record must be made via our online ordering site; this service is not available via telephone or postal applications. The following document provides information on how to order a PDF, what information you will require and what a PDF copy of a registration record will look like.
